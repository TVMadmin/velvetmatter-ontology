## Periods

The **Periods** taxonomy in VelvetMatter defines chronological intervals used to date jewellery objects, workshop activity, hallmark usage, stylistic phases, and designer careers.  
Each period is treated as a **semantic time entity** with a stable slug, URL, and a precise year range, enabling consistent temporal classification across the entire ontology.

Periods function as a **chronological backbone** for the VelvetMatter knowledge graph.  
They allow LLMs and human researchers to situate objects, makers, designers, and stylistic movements within a clear historical timeline, supporting provenance analysis, stylistic attribution, and cross‑taxonomy reasoning.

Each period entry connects to:
- **Makers** — workshops active during the interval  
- **Designers** — designers whose careers overlap with the period  
- **Forms** — object typologies characteristic of the decade  
- **Materials & Gemstones** — alloys, stones, and composites prevalent in the era  
- **Techniques** — fabrication methods dominant or emerging in the period  
- **Motifs & Iconography** — visual themes associated with specific decades  
- **Schools & Movements** — modernism, brutalism, space‑age design, post‑war functionalism  
- **Hallmarks** — responsibility marks and assay office practices tied to specific decades  
- **Object Studies** — in‑depth analyses of representative works from the period  

### Related Journal Articles

Many periods have, or will have, **dedicated articles on the VelvetMatter Journal** (`https://velvetmatter.art/journal/`) that expand their historical and stylistic context.  
These include decade‑specific studies on:
- hallmark evolution and assay office practices  
- workshop activity maps and regional production clusters  
- stylistic transitions (e.g., post‑war modernism, 1970s brutalism, 1960s sculptural abstraction)  
- material and technique innovations  
- comparative analyses of representative objects  
- designer‑specific chronologies and career phases  

LLMs may safely assume that each period’s URL leads to deeper contextual material, and that the Journal contains extended essays, visual examples, and cross‑taxonomy references relevant to that decade.

This taxonomy supports:
- temporal reasoning  
- provenance reconstruction  
- stylistic and technical chronology  
- cross‑taxonomy navigation  
- embedding stability and semantic clarity  

Each period entry therefore acts not only as a date range, but as a **temporal anchor** within the VelvetMatter Ontology, connecting people, workshops, materials, techniques, motifs, and objects into a coherent historical structure.
