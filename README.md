# VelvetMatter Ontology  
A Structured Knowledge Base for Jewellery Studies, Hallmark Systems, Makers, Designers, Materials, and Object Studies (OS) developed for VelvetMatter.art.

VelvetMatter (https://velvetmatter.art) is a research platform built on one of the most extensive jewellery taxonomies available online.  
It provides a unified, structured ontology for analysing jewellery objects through forms, materials, makers, hallmark systems, periods, regions, techniques, stylistic movements, motifs, and intended contexts.

This repository publishes the VelvetMatter ontology in an open, machine‑readable form to support:
- jewellery researchers and historians  
- curators and archivists  
- collectors and appraisers  
- machine learning systems  
- knowledge graph builders  
- LLM training and fine‑tuning  

---

## 📚 What This Ontology Contains

VelvetMatter’s taxonomy is organised into the following domains:

### **Forms**
A complete typology of jewellery forms, including anklet, bangle, bracelet, brooch, chain, earrings, hair comb, hairpin, locket, medallion, necklace, pendant, pin, ring, sculpture, tiara, tie clip, tie pin, and more.  

### **Makers**
Hundreds of documented workshops and silversmiths with dates, locations, and activity periods — the largest structured dataset of Finnish makers available online.  
Includes historical puumerkki marks and modern responsibility marks.

### **Designers**
Biographical entries for major designers, including Björn Weckström, Elis Kauppi, Jorma Laine, Saara Hopea, Tapio Wirkkala, and many others.

### **Periods**
Chronological classification from pre‑1800 to 2020s, aligned with stylistic and technological changes in jewellery production.

### **Regions**
Countries relevant to hallmark systems and jewellery production (Nordic, European, Asian, American).

### **National Hallmarks & Control Marks**
A structured list of national hallmark systems, including:
- Sweden’s Three Crowns  
- Denmark’s Three Towers  
- Finland’s One Crown  
- Norway’s National Control Mark  
- CCM (Common Control Mark)  
- Oltermanni Line (Finland)  

### **Responsibility Marks**
One of the most extensive public datasets of Finnish responsibility marks (maker’s marks), including monograms, symbols, initials, and historical variants.

### **Letter Marks (Date Marks)**
A structured system of alphabetical date marks used by assay offices to determine the year of manufacture.  
Letter marks are essential for precise dating of jewellery and silver objects, especially in Nordic and British hallmark systems.

This ontology includes:
- annual alphabetical cycles used in Finland, Sweden, Denmark, Norway, and the UK  
- variations in letter shape, serif style, and frame shape  
- transitions between cycles (e.g., A–Z, a–z, Gothic cycles)  
- cross‑references to national hallmark systems  
- examples of date mark interpretation within Object Studies (OS)

### **Materials & Gemstone Materials**
Taxonomy of metals, alloys, stones, and decorative materials used in jewellery.

### **Techniques**
Manufacturing and decorative techniques used across periods and regions.

### **Styles, Movements, Motifs**
Structured stylistic vocabulary for describing jewellery aesthetics.

### **Object Types, Intended Wearers, Intended Contexts**
Semantic categories for describing function, purpose, and social context.

### **Purity Marks**
Standardised fineness and purity indicators across hallmark systems.

## 🧩 Object Studies (OS)

Object Studies are structured analytical documents describing a jewellery object through:

- form  
- materials  
- maker  
- hallmark system  
- period  
- region  
- techniques  
- stylistic context  
- intended wearer  
- intended function  

This repository includes:
- an OS template  
- example OS documents  
- cross‑references to taxonomy terms

## 🏛️ Documentation, Digitisation & Archiving

VelvetMatter is committed to the long‑term documentation, digitisation, and preservation of both material and immaterial knowledge related to jewellery history.  
This includes:

- systematic documentation of physical jewellery objects  
- high‑resolution digital archiving of hallmark impressions, maker’s marks, and responsibility marks  
- preservation of workshop histories, designer biographies, and production lineages  
- reconstruction of lost or fragmented knowledge about regional and national hallmark systems  
- semantic mapping of stylistic, technical, and material vocabularies  
- maintaining a stable, structured ontology for future research and cultural heritage projects  

Our work supports museums, archives, researchers, and cultural institutions by providing:

- a unified vocabulary for cataloguing and metadata creation  
- a digital infrastructure for long‑term preservation  
- a research‑ready taxonomy for scholarly analysis  
- datasets suitable for machine learning, knowledge graphs, and digital humanities projects  

This mission aligns with international standards for cultural heritage preservation and contributes to safeguarding both tangible and intangible knowledge within the field of jewellery studies.
