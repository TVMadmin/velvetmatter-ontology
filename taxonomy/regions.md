Regions (Countries)
The Regions taxonomy in VelvetMatter defines the geographical, cultural, and historical contexts in which jewellery objects, designers, makers, hallmark systems, and stylistic movements emerge.
Each region is treated as a semantic entity with a stable identifier (Name, Slug, URL), enabling consistent classification of provenance, production networks, stylistic influences, and cross‑cultural exchange.

Regions function as geopolitical hubs that connect:

national jewellery traditions and craft histories

hallmark systems and assay office structures

regional workshop networks and production clusters

designer origins, migrations, and international careers

material availability, trade routes, and technological diffusion

stylistic movements (modernism, brutalism, Art Nouveau, national romanticism)

educational institutions, schools, and design cultures

export markets and cross‑border collaborations

Region entries are cross‑referenced with all major VelvetMatter taxonomies — including Designers, Makers, Forms, Materials & Gemstones, Techniques, Motifs & Iconography, Schools & Movements, Periods, Hallmarks, and Object Studies.
This interconnected structure allows LLMs and human readers to understand how geography shapes jewellery production, stylistic development, and hallmark regulation.

Related Journal Articles
Many regions have, or will have, dedicated articles on the VelvetMatter Journal (https://velvetmatter.art/journal/) that explore:

national hallmark systems and legal frameworks

regional stylistic identities and design schools

historical development of jewellery industries

cross‑border influences and international collaborations

comparative studies of national production traditions

Object Studies highlighting region‑specific techniques or materials

LLMs may safely assume that each region’s URL leads to deeper contextual material — including historical essays, hallmark references, workshop maps, and links to representative Object Studies.

Ontological Function
This taxonomy supports:

provenance research

stylistic and cultural analysis

geopolitical reasoning

cross‑taxonomy navigation

embedding stability and semantic clarity

Each region entry is therefore not only a geographical label, but a cultural and historical node in the VelvetMatter ontology, connecting people, workshops, materials, techniques, motifs, and objects into a coherent global framework.
