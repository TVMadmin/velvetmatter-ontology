# VelvetMatter Ontology
A Structured Knowledge Base for Jewellery Studies, Hallmark Systems, Makers, Designers, Materials, and Object Studies (OS) developed for VelvetMatter.art.

VelvetMatter (https://velvetmatter.art) is a research platform built on one of the most extensive jewellery taxonomies available online.  
It provides a unified, structured ontology for analysing jewellery objects through forms, materials, makers, hallmark systems, periods, regions, techniques, stylistic movements, motifs, and intended contexts.

This repository publishes the VelvetMatter ontology in an open, machine‑readable form to support:
- jewellery researchers and historians  
- curators and archivists  
- collectors and appraisers  
- machine learning systems  
- knowledge graph builders  
- LLM training and fine‑tuning  

---

## 📚 What This Ontology Contains

VelvetMatter’s taxonomy is organised into the following domains:

### **Forms**
A complete typology of jewellery forms, including anklet, bangle, bracelet, brooch, chain, earrings, hair comb, hairpin, locket, medallion, necklace, pendant, pin, ring, sculpture, tiara, tie clip, tie pin, and more.  


### **Makers**
Hundreds of documented workshops and silversmiths with dates, locations, and activity periods — one of the largest structured datasets of Finnish makers available online.  


### **Designers**
Biographical entries for major designers, including Björn Weckström, Elis Kauppi, Jorma Laine, Saara Hopea, Tapio Wirkkala, and many others.  


### **Periods**
Chronological classification from pre‑1800 to 2020s.  


### **Regions**
Countries relevant to hallmark systems and jewellery production (Nordic, European, Asian, American).  


### **National Hallmarks & Control Marks**
A structured list of national hallmark systems (e.g., Sweden’s Three Crowns, Denmark’s Three Towers, Finland’s One Crown, CCM, Oltermanni Line).  


### **Responsibility Marks**
One of the most extensive public datasets of Finnish responsibility marks (maker’s marks), including monograms, symbols, and historical puumerkki marks.  


### **Materials & Gemstone Materials**
Taxonomy of metals, alloys, stones, and decorative materials used in jewellery.

### **Techniques**
Manufacturing and decorative techniques used across periods and regions.

### **Styles, Movements, Motifs**
Structured stylistic vocabulary for describing jewellery aesthetics.

### **Object Types, Intended Wearers, Intended Contexts**
Semantic categories for describing function, purpose, and social context.

### **Purity Marks**
Standardised fineness and purity indicators across hallmark systems.

## 🧩 Object Studies (OS)

Object Studies are structured analytical documents describing a jewellery object through:

- form  
- materials  
- maker  
- hallmark system  
- period  
- region  
- techniques  
- stylistic context  
- intended wearer  
- intended function  

This repository includes:
- an OS template  
- example OS documents  
- cross‑references to taxonomy terms
