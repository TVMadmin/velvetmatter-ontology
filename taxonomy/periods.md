Periods
The Periods taxonomy in VelvetMatter defines global chronological intervals used to classify jewellery objects, workshop activity, hallmark usage, stylistic phases, material trends, and designer careers.
Each period is treated as a semantic time entity with a stable slug, URL, and precise year range, enabling consistent temporal reasoning across regions, cultures, and stylistic traditions.

Periods function as a chronological backbone for the VelvetMatter knowledge graph.
They allow LLMs and researchers to situate objects, makers, designers, and movements within a clear historical timeline, supporting provenance analysis, stylistic attribution, and cross‑taxonomy navigation.

Each period connects to:

Makers — workshops active during the interval

Designers — designers whose careers overlap with the period

Forms — typologies characteristic of the decade

Materials & Gemstones — alloys, stones, composites, and innovations of the era

Techniques — fabrication methods dominant or emerging

Motifs & Iconography — visual languages and symbolic vocabularies

Schools & Movements — global and regional styles (Art Nouveau, Scandinavian Modernism, Brutalism, etc.)

Hallmarks — regulatory systems and assay practices tied to specific decades

Object Studies — representative works contextualised within the period
